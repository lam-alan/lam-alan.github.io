From A Beginner's Perspective

</About Me>

I'm Alan Lam. At the time of this writing, I am a third-year Math-Computer Science student at UCSD. As likely as it may seem, I did not really learn how to make websites by taking computer science courses. There was one lab in the Software Engineering course that went over making websites, but I didn't really learn much from that.

It's May 2018 right now. I learned how to make a website in January 2018. I wouldn't exactly call myself an expert when I've only been doing this for about 5 months. But I do have a basic understanding of the language and components of a website. I did build this website from scratch after all. It may not be fancy and flashy - nor may it follow traditional web development standards - but it works.

HTML (Hypertext Markup Language and CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) are the two main components for building a website. I learned how to work with both of them by taking classes on Codecademy, an online platform that offers free coding classes. Once I understood the basics, I applied the concepts and made a website. Yeah, I just jumped right in. It wasn't a flawless transition - as hardly anything ever is - but there is a learning curve to web development, and, for me, jumping right in was getting over the curve.

Photo Credit: Lan Wang

The third main component for building a website is JavaScript, which is a programming language. This is usually what makes websites fancy and flashy. This is also another reason why I would not consider myself an expert. I haven't learned how to work with it yet.

I'm still updating and maintaining the first website I created. That sounds a little sentimental, which was unintentional, but it kind of is in a way. It was the first real website I created on my own. It was when I was able to confidently say, "I know how to make a website". Maintaining that website, which is a portfolio of my hobbies, is, well, one of my hobbies. While I do not expect people to visit it - or find it through a Google search much less - I still treat it as if people are visiting it. So I am still learning little things here and there to improve it.

This website is the second website I've created. This website is the result of roughly 5 months of learning and building. Being a student at the time, the total number of hours is actually less than 5 months. Whether that's impressive or not, I would say that the time it takes to learn isn't that long. Granted, having programming experience helps immensely, but you don't really need to know how to code to make a website.

With that said, welcome to this website. Made by a beginner.

Alan Lam
